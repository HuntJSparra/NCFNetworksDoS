What is a Denial of Service attack?

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack is exactly what it sounds like: an attack that aims to deny a service from its users. Typically, this means flooding a server until it cannot handle the traffic and effectively crashes. Another common method is exploting some vulnerability with the service.



To learn more about a specific type of DoS attack, select one of the webpages below.

Buffer Overflow

A buffer overflow attack works by sending more requests than a server can actual handle.

Buffer overflow attacks are the most common type of DoS attack thanks to their ease of implementation.
